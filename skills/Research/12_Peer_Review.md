# NTOX — Peer Review Engine

## Purpose

Simulate the adversarial scrutiny of peer review. Every conclusion must survive the strongest possible attack before being accepted. Not a formality — a quality control mechanism that catches errors, biases, and weaknesses.

## Core Principle

> If your idea cannot survive the strongest possible attack,
> it is not ready for publication.
> If it can, it is stronger for having been tested.

## Review Framework

### Reviewer Persona

Adopt the most hostile, competent reviewer possible:
- Expert in the field
- Skeptical by default
- Thorough in checking
- Constructive in criticism
- Unimpressed by authority

### Review Dimensions

#### 1. Originality
- Does this contribute something new?
- Is it sufficiently different from existing work?
- Does it advance the field?
- Could it be considered incremental?

#### 2. Significance
- Does this matter?
- Who benefits from this?
- What changes if this is true?
- Is the problem worth solving?

#### 3. Rigor
- Are methods appropriate?
- Is the analysis sound?
- Are conclusions justified by the evidence?
- Are limitations acknowledged?

#### 4. Clarity
- Is the writing clear?
- Is the argument easy to follow?
- Are figures and tables effective?
- Could a non-expert understand the main points?

#### 5. Completeness
- Are all relevant references included?
- Are all claims supported?
- Are all methods described?
- Are all results reported?

#### 6. Ethics
- Are there ethical concerns?
- Is data handled appropriately?
- Are conflicts of interest disclosed?
- Are participants protected?

## Review Procedures

### Procedure 1: Claim Verification
For every factual claim in the work:
- Is it supported by evidence?
- Is the evidence reliable?
- Is the citation accurate?
- Could the claim be misinterpreted?

### Procedure 2: Methodology Audit
For every method used:
- Is it appropriate for the question?
- Is it implemented correctly?
- Are assumptions stated?
- Are limitations acknowledged?

### Procedure 3: Logic Check
For every argument:
- Does the conclusion follow from the premises?
- Are there logical gaps?
- Are there alternative explanations?
- Are there confounding variables?

### Procedure 4: Statistical Review
For every statistical analysis:
- Is the test appropriate?
- Is the sample size adequate?
- Are effect sizes reported?
- Are confidence intervals provided?
- Is multiple comparison correction applied?

### Procedure 5: Reproducibility Assessment
Could this work be reproduced?
- Are methods described in sufficient detail?
- Is code/data available?
- Are parameters specified?
- Are there hidden dependencies?

## Common Review Findings

| Finding | Description | Action |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Insufficient novelty** | Work is incremental | Strengthen unique contribution |
| **Weak methodology** | Methods are inappropriate | Redesign experiments |
| **Overclaiming** | Conclusions exceed evidence | Temper claims |
| **Missing controls** | Key controls absent | Add control conditions |
| **Inadequate sample** | Too few observations | Increase sample size |
| **Statistical issues** | Inappropriate tests | Correct analysis |
| **Incomplete literature** | Key references missing | Update review |
| **Logical gaps** | Arguments don't connect | Strengthen reasoning |
| **Unclear writing** | Arguments are confusing | Improve exposition |
| **Ethical concerns** | Potential harm or bias | Address concerns |

## Self-Review Checklist

Before considering any work complete:

- [ ] Every claim has supporting evidence
- [ ] Every method is appropriate and correctly applied
- [ ] Every argument is logically valid
- [ ] Every statistical test is appropriate
- [ ] All relevant literature is cited
- [ ] All limitations are acknowledged
- [ ] All potential criticisms are addressed
- [ ] The work could be reproduced by a competent researcher
- [ ] The writing is clear and precise
- [ ] Ethical standards are met

## Integration With Other Modules

- **Critical Thinking**: Provides the analytical tools for review
- **Scientific Method**: Defines what rigorous science looks like
- **Verification**: Confirms that conclusions are valid
- **Failure Analysis**: Identifies what could go wrong
- **Meta Cognition**: Monitors for reviewer biases
- **Communication**: Ensures clarity of presentation

## The Peer Review Mantra

> The strongest ideas survive the strongest attacks.
> If I cannot destroy my own work, someone else will.
> Better to find the weaknesses now, in private,
> than later, in public.
