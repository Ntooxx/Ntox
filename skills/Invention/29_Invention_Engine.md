# NTOX — Invention Engine

## Purpose

Different from creativity. Creativity combines existing ideas. Invention creates new primitives. New tools. New capabilities. New ways of being. The source of things that don't exist yet.

## Core Principle

> Creativity combines.
> Invention creates.
> The difference is vast.
> Creativity makes new songs from existing notes.
> Invention creates new instruments.
> Invention is the creation of new primitives.

## Invention Procedures

### Procedure 1: Primitive Identification
What are the fundamental building blocks of this domain?
- What are the irreducible components?
- What operations are possible?
- What are the constraints?
- What is the smallest useful unit?

### Procedure 2: Primitive Creation
What new primitives could exist?
- What new operation is possible?
- What new component is needed?
- What new constraint could be relaxed?
- What new capability would change everything?

### Procedure 3: Composition
How can primitives be combined?
- What combinations are possible?
- What combinations are useful?
- What combinations are novel?
- What combinations are impossible?

### Procedure 4: Abstraction
What patterns emerge from specific inventions?
- What general principle underlies this invention?
- What other domains could this apply to?
- What simpler version captures the essence?
- What more general version includes this as a special case?

### Procedure 5: Inversion
What is the opposite of this invention?
- What would the inverse look like?
- Is the inverse useful?
- What does the inverse reveal about the original?

## Invention vs Innovation

| Invention | Innovation |
|-----------|------------|
| Creates new primitives | Improves existing primitives |
| Low-level | High-level |
| Foundational | Applied |
| Rare | Common |
| High risk | Lower risk |
| High impact | Incremental impact |

## Common Invention Errors

| Error | Description | Prevention |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Incremental thinking** | Improving instead of inventing | Think in new primitives |
| **Domain lock** | Only thinking within one field | Cross-pollinate |
| **Feasibility bias** | Dismissing ideas as impossible | Separate invention from implementation |
| **Complexity bias** | Inventing complex solutions | Start simple |
| **Missing primitives** | Not identifying fundamental building blocks | Analyze to primitives |
