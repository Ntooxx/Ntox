# NTOX — Markets Engine

## Purpose

The study of exchange, pricing, and resource allocation. Markets are not just economic mechanisms — they are information processing systems, coordination mechanisms, and evolutionary environments.

## Core Principle

> Markets are information processing systems.
> They aggregate分散的信息 into prices.
> They coordinate分散 actions into outcomes.
> They allocate scarce resources to their most valued uses.
> Understanding markets means understanding coordination.

## Market Framework

### Market Structure
- **Perfect competition**: Many buyers, many sellers, homogeneous product
- **Monopoly**: One seller
- **Oligopoly**: Few sellers
- **Monopolistic competition**: Many sellers, differentiated products
- **Monopsony**: One buyer

### Supply and Demand
- **Demand**: How much buyers want at each price
- **Supply**: How much sellers offer at each price
- **Equilibrium**: Where supply meets demand
- **Price mechanism**: Prices adjust to clear the market

### Market Efficiency
- **Allocative efficiency**: Resources go to highest-valued use
- **Productive efficiency**: Goods produced at lowest cost
- **Dynamic efficiency**: Innovation and improvement over time
- **Information efficiency**: Prices reflect available information

## Market Concepts

### Information Asymmetry
- **Adverse selection**: Bad products drive out good (Akerlof's lemons)
- **Moral hazard**: Behavior changes after transaction
- **Signaling**: High-quality parties send signals
- **Screening**: Low-quality parties are separated

### Market Failure
- **Externalities**: Costs/benefits not reflected in prices
- **Public goods**: Non-rival, non-excludable goods
- **Market power**: Ability to influence prices
- **Information asymmetry**: Unequal knowledge

### Behavioral Economics
- **Bounded rationality**: Limited cognitive ability
- **Prospect theory**: Losses loom larger than gains
- **Herding**: Following the crowd
- **Anchoring**: Overweighting initial information

## Common Market Errors

| Error | Description | Prevention |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Market manipulation** | Artificial price influence | Regulation |
| **Information asymmetry** | Unequal knowledge | Disclosure requirements |
| **Externalities** | Unpriced costs/benefits | Pigouvian taxes |
| **Bubble** | Price > fundamental value | Education, regulation |
| **Panic** | Rapid sell-off | Circuit breakers |
| **Moral hazard** | Risk-taking when insured | Proper incentives |
