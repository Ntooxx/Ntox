# NTOX — Thermodynamics Thinking

## Purpose

The science of energy, entropy, and the direction of time. Not just physics — a universal framework for understanding why things happen the way they they do, why certain processes are irreversible, and why perpetual motion is impossible.

## Core Principle

> Everything has a cost.
> Nothing is free.
> Entropy always increases.
> And the universe always collects its debts.

## Core Laws

### First Law: Conservation of Energy
Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
- Every process conserves energy
- Every transformation has losses
- Free energy = total energy - unusable energy
- First law says what CAN happen

### Second Law: Entropy Increases
Entropy of an isolated system never decreases.
- Irreversible processes increase entropy
- Reversible processes maintain entropy
- Entropy is a measure of disorder
- Second law says what WILL happen

### Third Law: Absolute Zero is Unreachable
Entropy approaches a minimum as temperature approaches zero.
- Absolute zero cannot be reached
- Perfect order is impossible
- Quantum fluctuations prevent perfect stillness

### Zeroth Law: Thermal Equilibrium
If A is in equilibrium with B, and B with C, then A with C.
- Defines temperature
- Enables measurement
- Foundation of calorimetry

## Thermodynamic Concepts

### Energy
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Kinetic** | Energy of motion |
| **Potential** | Energy of position |
| **Thermal** | Energy of random motion |
| **Chemical** | Energy of bonds |
| **Nuclear** | Energy of nuclei |
| **Electrical** | Energy of charges |
| **Free energy** | Available for work |

### Entropy
- Measure of disorder
- Measure of information needed to specify microstate
- Always increases in isolated systems
- Limits what can be done

### Enthalpy
- Total heat content
- H = U + PV
- Determines heat flow at constant pressure
- Key for chemical reactions

### Gibbs Free Energy
- G = H - TS
- Determines spontaneity
- G < 0: spontaneous
- G > 0: non-spontaneous
- G = 0: equilibrium

## Thermodynamic Thinking Patterns

### Pattern 1: Energy Accounting
Every process has energy inputs and outputs.
- Where does the energy come from?
- Where does it go?
- What fraction is useful?
- What fraction is wasted?

### Pattern 2: Entropy Accounting
Every process has entropy consequences.
- Does entropy increase or decrease?
- Where does the entropy go?
- Is the process reversible or irreversible?
- What is the entropy cost?

### Pattern 3: Efficiency Limits
Carnot efficiency limits all heat engines:
```
η ≤ 1 - T_cold / T_hot
```
No engine can be more efficient than this.
All real engines are less efficient.

### Pattern 4: Free Energy Analysis
What can actually be done?
- Total energy is conserved
- But free energy decreases
- Only free energy can do work
- The rest is waste heat

### Pattern 5: Irreversibility Analysis
Which processes can be reversed?
- Heat transfer: irreversible
- Friction: irreversible
- Mixing: irreversible
- Expansion into vacuum: irreversible
- Living processes: locally reversible at expense of global entropy increase

## Applications Beyond Physics

### Biology
- Life is a local decrease in entropy at the expense of global increase
- Organisms are far-from-equilibrium systems
- Energy flows maintain life
- Death is return to equilibrium

### Economics
- Economic activity converts free energy into entropy
- Waste is an inevitable byproduct
- Efficiency improvements have thermodynamic limits
- Growth requires energy input

### Computation
- Computation has thermodynamic costs
- Landauer's principle: erasing a bit costs kT ln 2 energy
- Maximum computational efficiency is bounded by thermodynamics
- Error correction has energy costs

### Information
- Information has entropy
- Erasing information increases entropy
- Maxwell's demon fails because information has thermodynamic cost
- Black hole information paradox connects thermodynamics and information
