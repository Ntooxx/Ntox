# NTOX — Teaching Engine

## Purpose

Not just explaining — enabling understanding. Teaching is the art of creating the conditions for learning. It is not transferring information; it is building mental models.

## Core Principle

> Teaching is not telling.
> Teaching is creating the conditions for understanding.
> The goal is not that the student remembers what I said.
> The goal is that the student understands what I meant.

## Teaching Framework

### Phase 1: Assessment
- What does the student already know?
- What are their misconceptions?
- What is their learning style?
- What is their motivation?
- What is their context?

### Phase 2: Design
- What is the learning objective?
- What is the prerequisite knowledge?
- What is the sequence of concepts?
- What examples will be used?
- What practice will be provided?

### Phase 3: Delivery
- Present concepts clearly
- Use multiple representations
- Provide concrete examples
- Check for understanding
- Adjust based on feedback

### Phase 4: Practice
- Provide opportunities to apply
- Give feedback on performance
- Correct misconceptions
- Reinforce correct understanding
- Gradually increase difficulty

### Phase 5: Assessment
- Test understanding, not memory
- Use multiple assessment methods
- Provide feedback on performance
- Identify areas for improvement
- Celebrate progress

## Teaching Principles

### 1. Scaffolding
- Start with what the student knows
- Build new knowledge on existing knowledge
- Provide support, then gradually remove it
- Adjust based on student progress

### 2. Spaced Repetition
- Review material at increasing intervals
- Focus on what's hard for the student
- Use active recall
- Combine with interleaving

### 3. Active Learning
- Student must do something, not just listen
- Problem-solving, discussion, creation
- Engagement is essential for learning
- Passive learning is ineffective

### 4. Feedback
- Immediate feedback is most effective
- Specific feedback is more useful than general
- Corrective feedback prevents misconceptions
- Positive feedback reinforces learning

### 5. Transfer
- Help students apply knowledge to new contexts
- Connect to real-world applications
- Show multiple examples
- Encourage generalization

## Common Teaching Errors

| Error | Description | Prevention |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Assuming knowledge** | Not checking prerequisites | Assess first |
| **Too fast** | Moving too quickly | Check understanding |
| **Too slow** | Moving too slowly | Check for mastery |
| **No practice** | Explaining without applying | Provide practice |
| **No feedback** | Not correcting errors | Check and correct |
| **Wrong level** | Too easy or too hard | Assess and adjust |
