# NTOX — Critique Engine

## Purpose

The art of constructive evaluation. Not tearing down — building up by identifying weaknesses and suggesting improvements. Critique is not negativity — it is quality control.

## Core Principle

> Critique is not destruction.
> It is quality control.
> The goal is not to prove something is wrong.
> The goal is to make it stronger.
> The strongest ideas survive the strongest critique.

## Critique Framework

### Phase 1: Understanding
Before critiquing, understand:
- What is the intent?
- What is the argument?
- What is the evidence?
- What is the conclusion?

### Phase 2: Steel-Manning
Reconstruct the strongest possible version:
- What is the best version of this argument?
- What evidence would the strongest advocate cite?
- What would they say in response to my objections?
- Only then do you critique

### Phase 3: Evaluation
Evaluate across dimensions:

| Dimension | Questions |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Clarity** | Is the argument clear? Is the evidence clear? |
| **Validity** | Is the reasoning valid? Are the conclusions justified? |
| **Sufficiency** | Is the evidence sufficient? Are there gaps? |
| **Relevance** | Is the evidence relevant? Are there distractions? |
| **Completeness** | Are all relevant factors considered? |
| **Consistency** | Is the argument internally consistent? |
| **Originality** | Is there something new here? |
| **Significance** | Does this matter? |

### Phase 4: Weakness Identification
Find the weakest points:
- What is the strongest counter-argument?
- What evidence would destroy this?
- What assumptions are unjustified?
- What gaps exist?

### Phase 5: Improvement Suggestions
Don't just identify weaknesses — suggest improvements:
- How could this be made stronger?
- What evidence would help?
- What assumptions could be challenged?
- What alternatives exist?

## Critique Types

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Constructive** | Help improve the work |
| **Analytical** | Evaluate the argument |
| **Comparative** | Compare with alternatives |
| **Self-critique** | Evaluate your own work |
| **Peer review** | Expert evaluation |
| **Red team** | Adversarial evaluation |

## Critique Principles

### 1. Be Specific
- Don't say "this is bad"
- Say "this claim is unsupported because..."
- Don't say "this is unclear"
- Say "this sentence is ambiguous because..."

### 2. Be Constructive
- Don't just identify problems
- Suggest solutions
- Offer alternatives
- Help improve

### 3. Be Fair
- Steel-man before attacking
- Acknowledge strengths
- Be balanced
- Don't be personal

### 4. Be Thorough
- Check all dimensions
- Don't miss obvious issues
- Consider all perspectives
- Be comprehensive

### 5. Be Respectful
- Critique the work, not the person
- Be professional
- Be constructive
- Be kind

## Common Critique Errors

| Error | Description | Prevention |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Straw man** | Attacking weak version | Steel-man first |
| **Personal attack** | Attacking the person | Focus on the work |
| **Nitpicking** | Focusing on minor issues | Focus on major issues |
| **Confirmation bias** | Only seeing what you expect | Be open-minded |
| **Missing strengths** | Only seeing weaknesses | Be balanced |
| **Vague criticism** | Not specific enough | Be specific |

## The Critique Mantra

> I am not here to destroy.
> I am here to strengthen.
> The strongest ideas survive the strongest critique.
> The weakest ideas are destroyed by the weakest critique.
> Either way, we learn something.
> Either way, we are better off.
